✦ Help & FAQ
Getting Started
Do I need an account?
No. Everything runs in your browser. Use 💾 Save to store sessions in browser local storage.
How do I save my work?
Click 💾 Save at the bottom, give it a name, and it's stored locally. Use 📂 Load to restore. Warning: clearing browser data will erase saves.
Can I undo changes?
Yes — press Ctrl+Z (or ⌘Z on Mac) to undo, and Ctrl+Y / Ctrl+Shift+Z to redo. Up to 50 steps are tracked. The ↩ ↪ buttons in the top-right of the panel also work. Note: continuous slider drags are debounced — each drag counts as one undo step.
Templates
What templates are available?
Four templates are available:

Modern — A colored name bar across the top with a vertical sidebar column on the left or right. Best for visually distinctive, design-forward resumes. The sidebar makes it less suitable for ATS-only submissions.

Standard — Clean single-column layout with no sidebar or header bar. The most ATS-friendly option — parsers read it linearly without ambiguity. Best for applications going through applicant tracking systems.

Sidebar — Two-column layout with a dedicated sidebar and no top name bar. Good for visually separating skills and credentials from work history. Less ATS-friendly due to the column structure.

Top Bar — Full-width colored header with a clean single-column body below. ATS-friendly like Standard, with added visual structure in the header. Bar height, name font, and alignment are adjustable under Spacing & Align → Name & Heading. Defaults to center alignment.
Which template is best for ATS?
Standard and Top Bar are the most ATS-friendly — both use a single-column body that applicant tracking systems parse cleanly and in order. Avoid Modern and Sidebar when submitting through automated systems, as multi-column layouts can cause parsers to misread the content sequence.
What's the difference between Modern's Name Bar and Sidebar?
The Name Bar is the horizontal colored bar at the top showing your name and contact info. The Sidebar is the vertical column on the left or right for skills, certifications, and other sections. In Design → Colors, "Name & Header Bar" controls the top bar color; "Sidebar" controls the column color. Both are unique to the Modern template.
Where does contact info appear on Modern?
By default it appears in the Name Bar. Uncheck Show contact in Name Bar under Design → Section Style → Contact Placement, then go to Sections → Contact to assign it to the Sidebar or Main Column instead.
Editing Content
Can I click directly on the resume to edit?
Yes. Hover over any text on the live preview — editable areas show a dashed outline. Click to type. Press Enter or click away to confirm.
How do I add or remove sections?
Go to the Sections tab. Drag ⠿ to reorder (Contact cannot be reordered), click ▾ to expand, use ▤ Main / ◧ Sidebar to move columns, and ✕ Remove to delete. The ＋ Add dropdown at the bottom of each group lets you add Summary, Education, Skills, Certifications, Text Block, or List sections. Note: Experience is locked to the Main column and cannot be moved to the Sidebar.
Can I reorder sections on the live preview?
Yes. Hover over any section on the live preview to reveal the ⠿ drag handle on the left. Click and drag it to reorder sections in place — the panel updates automatically and the scroll position is preserved.
Design & Typography
How do I change name bar alignment?
Spacing & Align → Name Bar Alignment — available for Modern and Top Bar templates. Choices are Left, Center, and Right. Top Bar defaults to Center; Modern defaults to Left.
Design tab → Typography. Three groups: Heading & Name, Contact, and Body Content. A live "Aa" specimen below each dropdown previews the typeface.
What does Body Text font size control?
Body Text applies to all content not covered by the specific controls above it — bullets, skills, certifications, education, summary, and custom text. Section Header, Company & Location, and Job Title & Date each have their own separate size controls.
How do I change accent / section header color?
Design → Colors → Main → left swatch (Section Header accent). This drives divider lines, skill tag borders, and highlighted elements.
Can I switch the panel between dark and light?
Yes — use the Theme ☀︎ / 🌙 toggle in the top-right of the panel. This only affects the editor UI, not the resume output.
Exporting
How do I export to PDF?
Click ⬇ Export PDF or press Ctrl+P / ⌘P. In the print dialog, set destination to Save as PDF. The panel hides automatically. Use Chrome or Edge for best results.
Does the zoom level affect the PDF?
No. The preview zoom (+ / − bar) only affects what you see on screen. The PDF always exports at 100% regardless.
Why does the preview show a page break line?
When your content exceeds one page, a dashed red line and page counter appear at the boundary so you can see exactly where the break falls. This is a preview-only guide — it does not appear in the exported PDF.
Known Limitations
No Word (.docx) export — PDF via browser print only.
No image upload — profile photos or logos cannot be added.
No cloud sync — sessions save to browser local storage only. Clearing browser data will erase them.
No import from existing files — content must be entered manually.
Section-level color overrides are not supported — colors apply globally.
PDF rendering varies by browser — Chrome/Edge recommended.
Mobile editing is limited — optimised for desktop/laptop screens.
No manual page break control — breaks are auto-calculated.
Tips
Save frequently with descriptive names (e.g. Resume_TPM_v2) to track versions.
Use Ctrl+Z freely — up to 50 undo steps are always available.
Use the Zoom + / − bar to inspect fine details without affecting the exported PDF.
For ATS-safe resumes, use Standard or Top Bar — single-column layouts that applicant tracking systems parse cleanly.
Template thumbnails reflect your current accent and bar colors in real time.
Reset All Colors and ↺ Reset Spacing restore defaults if you get lost.
Drag the ⠿ handle on any section in the live preview to reorder without opening the Sections panel.
